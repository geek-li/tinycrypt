CubeHash submission to the SHA-3 competition

First submission

In October 2008, I proposed CubeHash80+8/1+80–224, CubeHash80+8/1+80–256, CubeHash80+8/1+80–384, and CubeHash80+8/1+80–512 as SHA-3 candidates. My submission to NIST included
  • a specification and rationale,
  • an analysis of attacks,
  • an efficiency estimate,
  • a statement of features,
  • a statement of expected strength, and
  • an appendix on the complexity of generic attacks,
along with software and test vectors.

Second submission

In July 2009, responding to NIST clarifications of speed criteria and security criteria, I proposed a different selection of CubeHash parameters for SHA-3:
  • CubeHash160+16/32+160–224 for SHA-3–224,
  • CubeHash160+16/32+160–256 for SHA-3–256,
  • CubeHash160+16/32+160–384 for SHA-3–384-normal,
  • CubeHash160+16/32+160–512 for SHA-3–512-normal,
  • CubeHash160+16/1+160–384 for SHA-3–384-formal, and
  • CubeHash160+16/1+160–512 for SHA-3–512-formal.
CubeHash16/32 is approximately 16 times faster than CubeHash8/1. I submitted a document
  • CubeHash parameter tweak: 16 times faster
to NIST describing this change in detail.

In September 2009, I sent NIST an updated

  • specification and rationale,
  • analysis of attacks,
  • efficiency estimate,
  • statement of features, and
  • statement of expected strength, plus a
  • description of changes in the submission.

Third submission

In November 2010, responding to concerns regarding the short-message performance of CubeHash (particularly for packet authentication), I proposed a different selection of CubeHash parameters for SHA-3:
  • CubeHash128, defined as CubeHash16+16/32+32–128, for AHS128;
  • CubeHash160, defined as CubeHash16+16/32+32–160, for AHS160;
  • CubeHash224, defined as CubeHash16+16/32+32–224, for AHS224;
  • CubeHash256, defined as CubeHash16+16/32+32–256, for AHS256;
  • CubeHash384, defined as CubeHash16+16/32+32–384, for AHS384;
  • CubeHash512, defined as CubeHash16+16/32+32–512, for AHS512;
  • CubeHash512x, defined as CubeHash16+16/1+32–512, for AHS512x; and
  • CubeMAC128, defined as CubeHash16+16/32+32–128 where a 512-bit secret key is prepended to the input, for AMAC128.
I submitted a document
  • CubeHash parameter tweak: 10 times smaller MAC overhead
to NIST describing this change in detail.
